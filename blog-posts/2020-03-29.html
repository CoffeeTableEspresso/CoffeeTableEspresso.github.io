Horror Movies

I've been watching a bunch of horror movies lately, being in quarantine and all. Most people don't "get" horror movies I don't think.

Criticisms include "low budget", "predictable", "bad plot". In the end, it doesn't even matter though. None of those things make them less enjoyable. I'm not watching horror movies for any of those things. I'm watching them to get my heart pumping. To scare myself a little bit.

Take Would You Rather for example. 5 Minutes in, Blonde's brother is "quite sick". Doctor suggests a "game", where the "winner" would be "taken care of" (i.e. get her brother the bone marrow transplant he needs). Already, it's quite predictable where this is going. And yet, it doesn't matter.

I've known the brother for 10 minutes. He's played by a terrible actor, as is his sister. I know almost nothing about him or his disease. I don't care about him in the slightest. I'm here to watch his sister play Would You Rather with deadly stakes.

What Makes a Horror Movie "Good"?

In my opinion, if a horror movie gets your heart pounding, it's "good". In the same way that porn is "good" if it gets you hard/wet. Not "good" in any meaningful way, "good" in the sense that they do exactly what they were designed for.

In this sense, Human Centipede is a "good" movie. Low budget, mostly laughable acting (save the doctor, he really sells it), almost no plot save "I want to sew 3 people together for some reason".

And yet, it's a terrifying experience. No, it's not "medically possible", but some lunatic could still try it, likely killing every link in the centipede along the way.

Horror Movie Rules

People seem to by a different set of rules in horror movies. No one in their right mind would split up with their friends when lost in the forest in the middle of the night. No one would sleep over at a random strangers house in the middle of nowhere. No one ever thinks to phone the police. (This is actually pretty sensible, since the police in horror movies are all but useless anyways.)

It's within these insane rules that horror movies exist. In this insane dream world. My favourite two plots are "rich people torture poor people for their own amusement" and "lone group is picked off one-by-one by a serial killer". Both completely insane plots, that could happen in real life but definitely won't.

This is part of the fun of horror movies. It's like a dream. Some plot that scares you, but won't ever happen. You watch the characters act out the worst possible choices, slowly dying off one-by-one. You know their decisions are bad. You can only sit back and watch. You can't change anything. And you don't get to wake up.

Horror Movies I Actually Like

Hush is a good one. The setup: A lone, deaf girl in a cabin. A murderer shows up to torment her. She eventually outwits and kills him. It's a classic plot really. And yet it works so well.

Escape Room is another personal favourite. Sinister corporation gets people to play a deadly game of escape room. Players die off one-by-one. There's a nice plot twist at the end, too.

Conclusion

Honestly, a post where I openly compare horror movies to porn probably shouldn't be taken too seriously. Just like horror movies. They're not meant to be some cinematic masterpiece. They're just about giving you a good scare.
